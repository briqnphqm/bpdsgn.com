Context

What I worked on as a UI/UX Designer in Ingram Micro’s Marketing Department.

I worked on “Template-Repository” (TR for short) – Ingram Micro’s in-house marketing campaign creation tool used by 279+ marketing associates globally. This tool allows associates to create company-branded marketing campaigns and have them sent to the inbox of subscribers.

My work centered on accelerating campaign creation to increase the volume of hand-off-ready campaigns for our subscribers.

Problem

What is not working well in Template Repository and how can I help?

Ingram Micro associates need to deliver hand-off ready marketing campaigns, but technical constraints and workflow ambiguity hinders their productivity.

This reduces the number of polished campaigns to PMs and creates an inconvenient campaign design experience.

User Need:

A campaign building experience that is flexible and contains less ambiguous roadblocks.

Business Need:

Needs more ready-to-be handed off email marketing campaigns.

Why is this a problem?

Through 6+ user interviews with marketing associates. I discovered 3 common patterns that users mentioned when it came to their experience with Template Repository.

Can only view 1 template at once.

Marketing Associates can only view & select to edit 1 template of a campaign at a time.

They must also upload unfinished work to save their progress.

Monotonous Design.

Monotonous Design makes it difficult for users to locate their desired campaign.

Repetitive icons also make it hard to locate the desired campaign.

Lack of Fluid Task Transitions.

Disjointed transitions make it difficult for users to identify their current stage of work.

E.g. in-put pop-up to full-screen design interface.

With this…

How might we optimize the email marketing campaign creation experience that provides marketing associates with clarity & efficiency needed to deliver hand-off ready campaigns?

Goals

What does success look like in this project?

After uncovering key pain points in Template Repository, I identified 4 key goals that align with user needs and business objectives.

Lessen ambiguity

To improve workflow clarity & enhancing user recall throughout the marketing campaign design lifecycle.

Reduce the time

To create/edit marketing campaigns while maintaining a digestible interface.

Add Flexibility

Enhance workflow flexibility to make users feel less constrained while creating email marketing campaigns.

Campaigns

Increase the amount of hand-off ready marketing campaigns for PMs to approve.

Users

There are 2 main users of Template Repository. How do they use the product?

As mentioned earlier, marketing associates are the primary user and program managers are the secondary user of Template Repository. Despite using the same product, they use it for different purposes.

Primary user: Marketing Associate

Currently 279+ globally, they are non-designers, meaning that need a tool that allows them to easily create company-branded marketing campaigns.

Secondary User: Project Manager

Responsible for reviewing marketing campaigns. Once approved, PMs send them to Workfront. They need a process that allows them to search for completed campaigns faster.

Research & Ideation

Competitive Analysis: Viewing Marketing Tools used in the past.

During interviews, I asked marketing associates at Ingram Micro what tools that were used in the past to create marketing campaigns. I researched these tools and analyzed the pros and cons of each product.

Feasibility and organizing ideas based on impact.

After conducting a competitive analysis, I gathered a list of potential features to explore based on the goals that I defined earlier. I focused on features that could speed up the process of creating campaigns, while reducing task ambiguity.

Crafting 2 primary flows: Create Campaign & Search for Campaign

The two primary flows in this product are creating a new campaign, & searching for an existing campaign. I mapped out a flow to guide the designing phase, containing the possible features to explore.

User Testing

Boosting Clarity in the Campaign Creation Process.

When conducting user testing, I learned that user recall plays a major role on how long it takes for users to design marketing campaigns. It was also important to section off important steps to avoid overload.

User Testing Insights 1/3

6/6 users preferred filling out campaign details before working on marketing templates since it not only reduces space, but also provides a smooth transition. Users are more aware of the steps needed to complete before working on designing the templates.

Assumptions

Initially, I assumed that including details and have the templates appear automatically, would save time since everything appeared on one page.

User Testing Insights

This increased cognitive overload & was also ambiguous. Users felt surprised whenever they switched templates due to not knowing what was going to appear.

Customize your own campaign without using a pre-existing one.

Previously, I conducted user testing on a campaign that is pre-existing and already contains a fixed number of templates. To enhance flexibility in the design process, it was important to provide users the option to customize their own work, especially in a way that can be interpreted by non-designers.

User Testing Insights 1/2

5/6 users preferred having a “get started” CTA in the design canvas to activate the dropdown menu that allows the users to begin adding sections (e.g. Hero Banner). It enhanced clarity and reduced the feeling of emptiness as mentioned in Version B.

Assumptions

With an empty design canvas without the “Get Started” CTA button, I assumed that users knew that this area was meant for designing.

User Testing Insights

Users did not know that the empty canvas was meant for designing. They also did not know that the dropdown menu was meant to view/add template sections.

Browse for completed campaigns and save unfinished work as a draft.

Since users must upload their work to save their progress, it appears in the “Browse Campaigns” section for everyone to see. This makes it difficult, especially for PMs to differentiate between completed campaigns and those saved as a draft. It was important to distinguish between the two.

User Testing Insights 1/3

Being able to select the following templates to save as a draft made users feel less overwhelmed in the marketing campaign design phase. It granted more flexibility and assured them that their work will be saved.

Assumptions

By displaying 3 templates, users would assume that they would already be completed and ready to be sent off by PMs. As a result, they are likely to see campaigns that are unfinished. This would interrupt the workflow for PMs that need to send-off finished campaigns.

Final MVP Design
Use an existing campaign?

Users have the option to select a campaign that already contains available templates.

Enlarging button and including previews helps users recall their work progress.

Feel like customizing?

Users have the option to not use a campaign with fixed templates.

Able to browse through company-branded sections, creating flexibility for non-designers.

Search for the desired campaign (1) and edit drafts (2).

With an updated design system that aligns with company branding, users can browse and filter through the desired campaigns.

Users wanting to browse able to differentiate between submitted templates and drafts. Drafts can become edited and submitted for PM approval.

Create Shareable Link for PMs

PMs are able to create a shareable URL for campaign approval.

There is emphasis on submitted templates to avoid confusion with template drafts.

Takeaways

What did I learn from this experience?

Despite the time constraint given, I was satisfied by the design decisions I came up with for the marketing associates responsible for designing marketing campaigns. If given more time, I would include additional solutions on top of an updated design system to assist PMs with locating their desired campaigns. Additionally, I came across 3 learning experiences:

Be open to feedback, while leveraging research data to validate your designs.
Organize Potential Solutions based on impact.
Even the smallest design component can enhance user recall.
